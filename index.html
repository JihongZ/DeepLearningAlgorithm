Background Information

Diagnostic Classification Modeling

It is a statistical model used for diagnostic measurement, which aims to classify respondents according to multiple latent variables. It is also known as constrained version of latent class model.

For example, to measure different facilities for solving problems in a particular educational domain (e.g., "borrowing from whole-number part" in fraction substraction problmens)

Background Information

Posterior Predictive Model Checking

PPMC is a method used to evaluate the goodness-of-fit of a diagnostic model in the full Bayesian framework.

PPMC uses the posterior distribution of model parameters to predict what the observed data should look like following the analysis -- Rupp et al., 2010
Background Information

Posterior Predictive Model Checking

  • Step 1: fit the model to data
  • Step 2: generate predictive data sets
  • Step 3: find the location of observed data sets
Background Information

Limited information statistics

Compared to full-information statistics, limited-information statistics use marginal information of contingency tables.

For example, M2 statistics (Maydeu-Olivares & Joe, 2005) uses up-to-second order marginal probability to calculate model-data fit.
Background Information

Limited-information statistics M2

The limited-information statistic have been proven to have lower Type I error rates in simulation settings (e.g., Hansen et al., 2016)

Hypothesis
  • PPMC based on limited-information statistics could be potentially an alternative way for goodness-of-fit statistics under the framework of Bayesian Diagnostic Classification Modeling
  • The proposed method could be comparable to other information criteria such as WAIC, BIC in term of power
Objectives of the Research
  • Construct PPMC with limited-information indices in Bayesian LCDM
  • Simulation Study: Determine the performance of PPMC with limited-information indices under different conditions
  • Empirical Illustration: Investigate the usefulness of PPMC with limited-information statistics in real settings
Procedure of the proposed method
  • Step 1: both hypothesized DCM and the saturated model are fitted to observed responses
  • Step 2: Randomly draw the posterior predictive distribution of parameters to create predictive data sets
  • Step 3: compute descrepancy measure M2 using up-to-second order marginal probabilities for each data set
  • Step 4: Check model fit by comparing the empirical M2 distribution with Chi-sqaure distribution

Simulation Study settings
The goal of this study is to examine whthether the PPMC with M2 statistics can detect three types of Q-matrix misspecification (misspecification, underspecification, overspecification). In addition, to examine how sample size, attribute correaltions affect the performance.
Limitations or potential issues
  • This study did not examine the effect of sparseness on the proposed model. The idea is that this study focus on whether the proposal method could be compared to full-information statistics and other information criteria.
  • The test length factor may affect the power of the proposed method but we did not examine it.
  • There are multiple ways calculating limited-information statistics under the Bayesian framework.
  • Only up-to-bivariate statistics are used in this study which may limit the power of limited-information statistics.
